# USER_INTERVIEWS.md

## Interview 1

### Person
A.K. — Full-stack developer at a 10-person startup

### Quotes
- “We’re paying for way too many AI tools because everyone has their own preference.”
- “Nobody actually audits these subscriptions after the first month.”
- “We mostly optimize cloud costs, not AI tooling.”

### Most Surprising Insight
The team had overlapping subscriptions across ChatGPT, Claude, and Cursor without any centralized review.

### Design Changes
This pushed me to prioritize clear savings visibility and simple recommendation explanations.

## Interview 2

### Person
R.S. — Indie hacker building AI SaaS tools

### Quotes
- “I buy tools impulsively if they save time.”
- “I’ve probably forgotten half my subscriptions.”
- “I care more about workflow quality than tiny savings.”

### Most Surprising Insight
Users do not necessarily want the cheapest stack — they want the best value-to-productivity ratio.

### Design Changes
I avoided aggressive cost-cutting recommendations and added “already optimized” states.
